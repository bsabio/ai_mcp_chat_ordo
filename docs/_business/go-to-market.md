# Go To Market

## Objective

Define the first focused path to acquire, convert, and retain high-value organizational clients and serious individual trainees for the AI bottega, while using Studio Ordo as both proof and platform.

## Initial Hypothesis

The business should start as a narrow, founder-led practice focused on strategic AI advice, operator training, and workflow architecture. Product can compound later, but the fastest path to revenue and learning is high-value advisory and training work with selective supervised execution.

## Core Market Thesis

Do not sell generic AI development. Sell founder-led AI strategy and workflow architecture for organizations that have expensive human judgment trapped inside messy workflows, and sell rigorous AI operator training to individuals who want to work at a higher level.

## Best Initial Offers

### Organization Offer Name

AI Strategy and Workflow Architecture Sprint

### Organization Goal

Help a client identify where AI creates real leverage, redesign the workflow, and define the first high-value operating model or system.

### Organization Default Conversion Target

The chat should be optimized to convert strong organizational prospects into this offer by default.

### Organization Core Deliverables

- Workflow audit
- Bottleneck and leverage analysis
- Human-AI operating model
- Pilot specification or supervised implementation plan
- Adoption and enablement plan

### Individual Offer Name

AI Operator Training Intensive

### Individual Goal

Help a serious individual learn how to orchestrate AI agents, evaluate outputs, structure workflows, and work with professional discipline.

### Individual Default Conversion Target

The chat should route qualified individual prospects toward this offer or a related training path.

### Individual Core Deliverables

- Assessment of current skill and goals
- Training plan or intensive session
- Methods for orchestration and evaluation
- Repeatable workflow discipline
- Path toward apprentice-level contribution

## Simple Offer Path

### Organization Lane

1. Free consultation
2. AI Strategy and Workflow Architecture Sprint
3. Team training or supervised implementation
4. Advisory retainer

### Individual Lane

1. Free consultation or fit assessment
2. AI Operator Training Intensive
3. Ongoing training, mentorship, or apprentice pathway

## Priority Markets

### Primary

Professional services firms and founder-led SMBs in Newark and NYC.

### Secondary

Higher education and workforce institutions.

### Individual Market

Developers and technical professionals who want rigorous training in AI agent orchestration.

### Later

Mission-driven and civic organizations where the work aligns and the economics make sense.

- Internal research and synthesis workflows
- Proposal and document generation with review layers
- Client onboarding and intake systems
- Architecture and delivery planning support
- Team knowledge retrieval and decision support
- Policy, operations, or training copilots with auditability
- Individual upskilling in agent orchestration and evaluation discipline

## Geographic Strategy

### Newark

Use Newark as the identity anchor: credible, mission-aware, serious, and local.

### NYC

Use New York City as the buyer market: denser network, higher budgets, stronger professional services demand.

### Positioning Benefit

This creates a strong regional identity without sounding like a generic Manhattan AI consultancy.

## Acquisition Channels

- Founder network and existing teaching/professional relationships
- Story-led essays on judgment, workflow design, and AI adoption, published as footer-linked informational pages
- Case-study pages built around concrete client problems, published as footer-linked informational pages
- Workshops and private briefings for leadership teams
- Training-led content and examples for serious individual operators
- Direct outreach to carefully selected higher-value prospects in Newark and NYC

## Conversion Strategy

- Lead with a clear problem, not a broad AI promise
- Sell a sprint to organizations, not an open-ended consulting engagement
- Sell a defined training experience to individuals, not generic inspiration
- Show artifacts, workflows, and operating examples
- Use Studio Ordo as a live demonstration of the method
- Emphasize founder involvement and capability transfer

The simpler rule is: the site should move the right visitor from chat to the right next step with as little friction and ambiguity as possible.

Informational pages should support this conversion model from the footer, not compete with it from the header.

## Business Funnel Architecture

The business funnel is conversational rather than form-first:

1. Anonymous visitor enters through chat.
2. Chat reveals use case, sophistication, urgency, fit, and lane.
3. System identifies whether the visitor is an organizational buyer, individual trainee, or implementation-heavy development prospect.
4. System identifies high-intent moments and likely conversion friction.
5. Product asks the user to sign up and continue the same conversation when continuity becomes valuable.
6. Product asks for contact information or a consultation request only after value is demonstrated.
7. Dashboard turns those conversations into lead and market intelligence.
8. Founder follows up on the strongest signals and creates simple deals when warranted.

This architecture is critical to keeping a one-man consultancy lean.

## Product Features Required For Go To Market

### 1. Contact Capture

Progressive lead capture must happen inside the conversation rather than through a generic static form.

### 2. Conversation Intelligence

Anonymous conversations must be analyzed for patterns, objections, market demand, and missed conversion opportunities.

### 3. Lane Detection And Routing

The product must distinguish organizational demand from individual training demand and send each visitor into the right next-step flow.

### 4. Consultation Request And Deal Flow

Serious signed-in conversations must be able to become consultation requests and, when warranted, simple deals with draft scope and pricing.

### 5. Solo-Operator Dashboard

The dashboard must help one person prioritize leads, understand market behavior, and decide what to change in offers, prompts, or messaging.

### 6. Recommendation Engine

The system should recommend actions such as adjusting homepage copy, refining prompts, adding a service offer, or following up with a lead.

## Delivery Strategy

- Keep engagements narrow at first
- Prefer advisory and training before implementation
- Prefer one high-value workflow over broad transformation promises
- Systematize documentation so each engagement improves the method
- Use supervised apprentice execution only where it improves leverage without lowering standards
- Convert repeated patterns into reusable Studio Ordo modules over time

The product and service loop should compound: each consulting engagement should improve the operating model, and the operating model should improve the product.

## Retention Strategy

- Expand from one workflow into adjacent workflows
- Offer advisory retainers after a successful sprint or training engagement
- Build trust through reliability, clarity, and educational rigor
- Leave clients with usable systems and stronger internal capability

## Metrics To Track

- Qualified conversations per month
- Organization consultation to sprint conversion rate
- Individual consultation to training conversion rate
- Sprint to training or supervised implementation conversion rate
- Average contract value
- Retainer attachment rate after pilot
- Training repeat or continuation rate
- Number of reusable workflow patterns extracted into product modules

## Risks

- Positioning too broadly as another AI consultancy
- Taking on low-value build work that hides the firm's real differentiation
- Letting the individual training lane become too broad or too casual
- Doing too much bespoke implementation without capturing reusable patterns
- Leading with product before the service offer is clearly validated
- Spreading across too many client types too early
