# Choosing Your Primary: Gift, Trap, and Shadow

## The Three-Part Evaluation

Selecting a primary archetype is not just about which description sounds most like you. It is about which archetype produces the clearest, most honest, most effective signal for the specific audience you are serving with the specific work you have actually done.

The three-part evaluation framework:

**The Gift:** What advantage does this archetype give you — what does it let you communicate clearly that other archetypes could not?

**The Trap:** What is the failure mode — how does this archetype go wrong, and is that failure mode something you are at risk of?

**The Shadow:** What does this archetype look like when it is forced, misapplied, or used without congruence — and does your current work invite that misread?

## Gift, Trap, and Shadow for Each Archetype

| Archetype | Gift | Trap | Shadow |
|---|---|---|---|
| Innocent | Immediate, honest trust | Naivety that reads as inexperience | Performing simplicity as a brand without real capability |
| Explorer | Authentic curiosity, process transparency | Endless journey with no arrival | Seeming unfocused or unable to commit |
| Sage | Durable authority through evidence | Distance that converts rigor into inaccessibility | Pedantry that signals insecurity rather than expertise |
| Hero | Rapid trust from proven competence | Overstatement that collapses under scrutiny | Self-promotion without service that reads as ego |
| Outlaw | Moral clarity and memorable differentiation | Grievance without alternative | Contrarianism that signals instability rather than reform |
| Magician | Transformational positioning for complex work | Vague transcendence without mechanism | Mysticism that reads as charlatanism |
| Everyman | Relatable presence that reduces evaluation friction | Underselling expertise through excess relatability | Blandness that produces no memorable signal |
| Caregiver | Relational trust through demonstrated care | Selflessness without visible capability | Performing warmth as substitute for competence |
| Lover | Depth of appreciation that signals genuine quality | Indulgence without purpose or conversion | Aesthetic performance that never grounds in value |
| Jester | Relief from seriousness; accessible entry point | Tonal mismatch in high-stakes evaluation contexts | Anxiety masked as humor; avoidance of real claims |
| Creator | Craft integrity that signals quality through process | Craft for its own sake disconnected from audience value | Precious perfectionism that never ships |
| Ruler | Institutional confidence and standard-setting authority | Distance and rigidity that blocks human connection | Authority without service that reads as control |

## The Honesty Test

Before finalizing your archetype selection, answer these three questions honestly:

**Is there real evidence for the gift?** Not theoretical evidence — actual, deployable proof that you have done what the archetype promises. A Sage without real method to show is performing the archetype. A Hero without real accomplishments is overclaiming.

**Are you at risk of the trap?** Not in general — specifically, given your current work and current audience. If you know you tend toward the Magician's grandiosity or the Explorer's unfinished journeys, account for that structurally in the page design.

**Is the shadow visible in your current materials?** Look at your existing work with fresh eyes. Ask: does this read as a Hero, or does it read as a Hero-shadow (self-promotion without service)? Does it read as a Sage, or does it read as a Sage-shadow (inaccessible rigor)? Adjust before publishing.

## The One-Primary Rule Revisited

After running the three-part evaluation, the one-primary rule should feel less like an arbitrary constraint and more like a practical necessity.

Every archetype has a gift and a trap and a shadow. When you combine two primaries without a clear hierarchy, you combine two traps and two shadows along with two gifts. The reader cannot benefit from the gifts because the traps are competing and the signal is ambiguous.

One primary. One gift doing the work. The traps and shadows minimized by the specific constraints of the system. That is the stable configuration.
