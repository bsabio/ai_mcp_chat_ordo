# The Forward Deployed Engineer: The Role This Program Builds Toward

## What Forward Deployment Means

The term "Forward Deployed Engineer" was introduced by Palantir to describe engineers embedded with customers — not sitting at headquarters writing code, but in the customer's environment, building alongside the customer's team, understanding their real problems, and deploying working software into their specific operational context.

The role has since generalized across the AI industry, appearing in variations at Databricks, Anthropic, Tome, Scale AI, and in the public sector. The title varies: Forward Deployed AI Engineer, AI Product Engineer, Enterprise AI Engineer, Solutions Architect with Engineering Depth. The profile is consistent.

## The Profile

**Builds end-to-end AI systems.** Not just frontend, not just backend, not just prompts. RAG pipelines, agentic workflows, evaluation harnesses, production deployment, monitoring, and the iteration cycle. End-to-end ownership.

**Deploys into real organizational contexts.** Understands that the technical system is not the whole problem. Change management, stakeholder alignment, training the people who will use the system, and navigating organizational constraints are part of the job.

**Evaluates rigorously.** Can specify what "working" means for a given application domain, build the infrastructure to measure it, and make defensible claims about system quality — not just demonstrate that the demo looks good.

**Communicates across technical and non-technical audiences.** Can write a technical specification and explain it to an engineer. Can also explain the same system to a procurement officer, a compliance team, or a business leader who has never written code and needs to decide whether to trust the system with important decisions.

**Takes ownership of outcomes, not just outputs.** The deliverable is not code — it is a system that produces value for the people using it. If the system is technically correct but nobody uses it effectively, that is a failure. Adoption and real-world impact are part of the scope.

## What the First Real Job Looks Like

The first role for someone with this profile is likely one of:

- AI engineer at an AI-forward startup, with broad scope and high autonomy
- Forward deployed or solutions engineer at an AI infrastructure company, working directly with enterprise clients
- AI product engineer at a mid-size company building its first AI-native product team
- Data and AI consultant at a professional services firm, embedded with client teams

The common thread: broad scope, customer or stakeholder proximity, high expectation for independent judgment, and the need to show visible, documented results.

The second and third roles compound on the first. The proof of capability built in each engagement becomes the evidence base for the next. The portfolio grows with each deployment. The weak-tie network that forms around each project expands the reach for future opportunities.

This is the career architecture this curriculum is designed to support: not a credential that implies readiness, but a visible, documented track record of doing the work.
