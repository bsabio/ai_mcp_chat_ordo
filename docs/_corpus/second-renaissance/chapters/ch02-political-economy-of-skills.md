# The Political Economy of Skills

## When Symbolic Production Gets Cheap

The Renaissance can be described as a broad reorientation toward classical knowledge, new forms of inquiry, and new cultural production. The Reformation can be described as a reconfiguration of religious authority — not the disappearance of religion, but a decentralization of interpretive control.

A key mechanism in both was that print **multiplied access** to texts and enabled a rapid pamphlet sphere. German-speaking lands were awash in pamphlets. Reformation-era publications blended text with images and oral-readability. Diffusion depended on networks of literate opinion leaders as well as mass readership.

The insight that matters here is not just that more people had access to ideas. It's that **the categories of who was allowed to produce and validate knowledge became unstable**.

## AI Is Doing the Same Thing One Layer Deeper

The printing press democratized knowledge artifacts.

AI democratizes the production of competent output — drafts, code, analysis, explanation, design iterations.

When producing a passable output becomes cheap, the question shifts. It is no longer: "Can you do this?" It becomes: "Do you understand what you did, why it matters, and whether it worked?"

This shifts advantage toward:

- **Judgment**: knowing which problems are worth solving and how to evaluate whether solutions are working
- **Ethics and purpose**: understanding what responsible deployment looks like
- **Taste and synthesis**: being able to distinguish strong from weak, not just produce
- **Real-world deployment discipline**: being able to ship, present, measure, and iterate in actual organizational contexts

## The Labor Market Evidence

Major institutional analyses converge on three macro-patterns:

**Value creation is shifting toward AI-enabled workflows.** The estimated annual value creation from generative AI across use cases is measured in trillions of dollars. This signals durable adoption incentives, not a passing trend.

**Exposure is broad, not niche.** The IMF estimates approximately 40% of global employment is exposed to AI, and approximately 60% in advanced economies. This means AI literacy is becoming general workforce infrastructure, while advanced building skills become high-leverage differentiators.

**Demand for AI skills is observable and growing.** The Stanford AI Index reports measurable growth in job postings requiring AI skills — rising from 1.4% to 1.8% of all US postings from 2023 to 2024. The core skill clusters show up consistently: Python, computer science, data analysis, SQL, automation, cloud, agile, scalability.

## The Roles That Are Emerging

The labor market is converging on a specific profile. The title varies — Forward Deployed AI Engineer, AI Product Engineer, Enterprise AI Engineer — but the profile is consistent:

Someone who can build LLM systems, evaluate and operate them, and collaborate with humans to drive adoption and real-world outcomes. Not just a coder. Not just a product manager. A person who can close the loop from problem to production.

What Palantir, Databricks, Anthropic, Accenture, and city governments are all asking for in their posted roles is the same combination: end-to-end ownership, evaluation discipline, production-grade engineering, and strong human communication. The degree in this system is designed directly for this profile.

## The Institutional Implication

When symbolic production becomes cheaply reproducible, institutions that depend primarily on controlling access to standardized knowledge face pressure on their legitimacy.

The printing press created this pressure for churches and scholarly institutions in the 16th century. AI is creating the same pressure for credentialing systems today.

The institutions that survive this shift will be the ones that can prove value through **transformation** — coaching, community, authentic assessment, and developing the human capacities that remain scarce even when output production is cheap.

That is the argument for the kind of program and system described here. Not "we teach AI." But: "We rebuild your capacity to read, write, reason, build, and deploy — in a world where surface polish is easy and judgment is what is rare."
