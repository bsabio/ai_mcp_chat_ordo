# Services

## Objective

Define a service model that a one-man practice can deliver reliably, profitably, and repeatedly while using Studio Ordo as both proof and platform.

---

## 1. Service Thesis

Do not sell generic AI development.

Sell founder-led strategic AI advice, workflow architecture, operator training, and selectively supervised execution for organizations with expensive human judgment trapped inside messy workflows.

Also sell rigorous AI operator training for high-skill individuals who want to learn how to orchestrate AI agents with professional discipline.

The firm should be hired when a client needs someone who can:

1. Frame the real problem
2. Identify where AI creates leverage
3. Decide what should remain human
4. Teach people how to work with AI responsibly
5. Support execution only when it meaningfully follows from the strategy

---

## 2. Core Offer Ladder

### 2.1 Free Consultation

**Purpose:** Up to one hour to establish fit, understand the problem, and decide whether a structured engagement is warranted.

**What it is for:**

- Qualification
- Relationship start
- Fast problem framing
- Deciding whether to move into a paid strategy or training engagement

### 2.2 Strategic AI Advisory

**Purpose:** Help a leader or organization make good decisions about where AI fits, what operating model is needed, and what path should be taken.

**Deliverables:**

- Problem framing
- AI leverage and risk analysis
- Operating model guidance
- Prioritized next-step recommendation

**Why it exists:**

- Keeps the work anchored in judgment rather than commodity delivery
- Creates a clean first paid step when a full sprint is premature
- Establishes the founder as strategic advisor, not generic vendor

### 2.3 AI Strategy And Workflow Architecture Sprint

**Purpose:** The flagship initial engagement for organizations.

**Deliverables:**

- Workflow map
- Human-AI operating model
- Leverage analysis
- First pilot definition
- Recommendation memo and execution options

**Why it exists:**

- Converts ambiguous interest into a concrete project
- Provides a strong deliverable for founder-led consulting
- Creates the bridge to training, supervised implementation, or advisory

### 2.4 AI Operator And Developer Training

**Purpose:** Train individuals or teams to orchestrate AI agents, evaluate outputs, and work with stronger professional discipline.

**Typical outputs:**

- Half-day training engagement
- Full-day training engagement
- Focused operator intensive
- Team enablement workshop
- Apprentice-oriented training pathway

**Why it exists:**

- Capability transfer is one of the firm's strongest differentiators
- Training scales the founder's judgment better than broad build work
- It creates a clean lane for serious individuals, not just organizations

### 2.5 Supervised Implementation

**Purpose:** Deliver one meaningful next step after advisory or sprint work when implementation support is clearly justified.

**Typical outputs:**

- Internal research assistant
- Proposal workflow system
- Knowledge retrieval tool
- Client onboarding workflow
- Planning and recommendation workflow

**Delivery model:**

Implementation can include apprentice contributors, but standards, architecture, review, and client accountability remain founder-led.

**Why it exists:**

- Produces measurable value quickly
- Avoids broad transformation promises
- Creates reusable product insight for Studio Ordo

### 2.6 Advisory Retainer

**Purpose:** Support governance, iteration, and expansion after a successful sprint, training engagement, or implementation.

**Deliverables:**

- Prioritization support
- Roadmap guidance
- Workflow refinement
- Review of new use cases
- Executive-level decision support

**Why it exists:**

- Creates recurring revenue
- Keeps the work strategic rather than purely implementation-based
- Lets the firm grow client value without taking on full agency overhead

## 3. Best-Fit Clients

### Primary

Professional services firms and founder-led SMBs that need leverage in research, delivery, onboarding, and planning.

### Secondary

Higher education and workforce institutions where the founder's credibility and operating experience are strongest.

### Individual Lane

Developers and technical professionals who want rigorous training in AI agent orchestration and professional AI practice.

### Later

Mission-driven and civic organizations where the work aligns and the economics make sense.

---

## 4. Delivery Rules For A One-Man Practice

1. Keep scopes tight.
2. Prefer strategy and training before implementation.
3. Prefer one workflow over broad transformation.
4. Productize repeated patterns quickly.
5. Avoid custom work that does not teach the product or the method anything reusable.
6. Use apprentice labor only inside bounded, supervised delivery.
7. Make every engagement improve the dashboard, the prompts, the workflow model, or Studio Ordo itself.

---

## 5. What Not To Sell

1. Generic chatbot builds
2. Prompt engineering as a stand-alone service
3. Open-ended custom development without a workflow thesis
4. Low-ticket AI seminars with no path to serious skill-building
5. Broad digital transformation promises that exceed one-person capacity
6. Unsupervised subcontracted work

---

## 6. Role Of Studio Ordo

Studio Ordo should support the services business in six ways:

1. Demonstration: visitors see the consulting philosophy in action
2. Qualification: anonymous chat reveals fit, pain, intent, and whether the visitor belongs to the organization or individual lane
3. Deal formation: serious signed-in conversations can become consultation requests and, when warranted, structured deal records with draft scope and estimates
4. Training support: workflows, retrieval, planning, and recommendations can be used as teaching material and operating examples
5. Delivery: workflows, retrieval, planning, and recommendations can be shown or delivered inside the product
6. Intelligence: conversations, deals, and leads become business feedback for the founder

## 6A. Informational Surface Rule

The service model should be explained through footer-linked informational pages rather than header navigation.

This keeps the top of the product focused on conversation while still making offers, proof, and training detail easy to research.

## 7. Pricing Baseline

The current pricing baseline should be treated as structured estimation guidance rather than an automatic binding quote.

1. Human consulting time: `$300/hour`
2. Full-day training: `$2,000`
3. Half-day training: `$1,100`
4. Initial consultation: free for up to one hour

The system may use these numbers to draft an exploratory estimate, but admin review remains required before anything is treated as a formal commercial commitment.

## 8. Simple Service Rule

Keep the public model simple:

1. Start with a free consultation.
2. Route visitors into the organization lane or the individual training lane.
3. Move qualified organizational opportunities into advisory or the Architecture Sprint.
4. Move qualified individual opportunities into training.
5. Use supervised implementation only where it clearly follows from the higher-value work.
6. Offer advisory only where ongoing value is obvious.
